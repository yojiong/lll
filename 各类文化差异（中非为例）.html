相互依赖：打破群体偏见的关键纽带

        在探讨群体间关系时，种族歧视与刻板印象是不可忽视的重要议题。刻板印象作为一种对团体全体成员的概括性认知， 将相同特征无差别地应用于团体中的每一个人，且一旦形成，便极难因新信息而改变，尤其是针对外群体的刻板印象， 这背后存在着 “外群体同质性效应”，即人们倾向于认为外群体成员的相似度高于内群体成员，进一步加深了刻板印象的顽固性。 种族歧视者常以 “肤色” 等种族特征作为负面分类标准，贯彻其根深蒂固的负面情感与刻板认知。

         早期接触假说认为，种族歧视者产生偏见的根源在于与其他种族接触不够， 基于此，美国实施 “种族混合教育计划” 来改善种族歧视现象。然而，该计划执行几年后，黑人学生自尊不增反降。深入探究发现，这种 “混合” 方式过于简单粗暴，未能从根本上解决问题。 在接触过程中，两个群体并未处于平等地位，彼此间的敌意与不信任也未消解，导致在学校中不同种族学生仍各自抱团，无法真正实现融合。

        在此背景下，社会心理学家埃利奥特·阿伦森提出 让接触起到减少偏见作用的六个基本条件，其中“两个群体相互依赖”这一条件尤为关键。两个群体相互依赖，意味着当两个团体产生互相依赖性时，他们才有可能对彼此产生信任，强行让两个怀有敌意的群体共处只会加剧群体间的敌意与冲突。这一观点深刻揭示了群体关系改善的内在逻辑。

Interdependence: The Key Bond to Breaking Group Biases

When examining intergroup relations, racial discrimination and stereotypes are critical issues that cannot be ignored. Stereotypes, as generalized perceptions of all members of a group, indiscriminately apply the same characteristics to every individual within that group. Once formed, they are highly resistant to change, even in the face of new information—particularly stereotypes about outgroups. This persistence is reinforced by the "outgroup homogeneity effect," where people tend to perceive members of outgroups as more similar to one another than members of their own ingroup, further entrenching rigid stereotypes. Racists often use racial markers like "skin color" as a basis for negative categorization, reinforcing deep-seated prejudices and stereotypical beliefs. The contact hypothesis suggests that racial prejudice stems from insufficient interaction with other racial groups. Based on this, the U.S. implemented "racial integration in education" programs to combat discrimination. However, years later, Black students' self-esteem did not improve—in fact, it declined. Further analysis revealed that this "integration" approach was overly simplistic and failed to address the root issues. During contact, the two groups were not on equal footing, and mutual hostility and distrust remained unresolved, leading to continued racial segregation within schools rather than genuine integration. Against this backdrop, social psychologist Elliot Aronson proposed six key conditions for contact to effectively reduce prejudice, among which "interdependence between groups" is particularly crucial. Interdependency means that when two groups rely on each other, trust can develop between them. Forcing hostile groups into mere coexistence without mutual reliance only exacerbates intergroup conflict and animosity. This insight profoundly reveals the underlying logic of improving intergroup relations.

中非对比

        从艺术领域中国和南非的刻板印象与误解来看，这一观点同样具有重要意义。在音乐方面，对中 国音乐和南非音乐存在诸多刻板印象，如认为中国音乐主要与古代乐器相关且缺乏现代影响，南非音乐只是传统非洲节奏和部落声音的简单呈现。这些刻板印象限制了人们对两国音乐文化多元 性的理解，阻碍了文化交流。若要打破这些刻板印象，促进音乐文化交流，就需要两国在音乐领域建立相互依赖的关系。例如，通过合作举办音乐活动，让两国音乐家相互学习、共同创作，在这 个过程中，双方会逐渐发现彼此音乐文化的丰富内涵，进而产生信任，打破原有的刻板认知。

        在舞蹈领域，南非传统舞蹈被视为原始和过时，中国传统舞蹈被认为刻板且缺乏情感，现代舞蹈在两 国也面临不被认可等问题。要改变这种状况，两国舞蹈界可以开展合作项目，共同编排融合两国舞蹈特色的作品。在合作过程中，双方必然会相互依赖，共同克服创作、表演过程中的困难，这种 相互依赖会促使双方放下偏见，增进对彼此舞蹈文化的理解与欣赏。

        文学和影视领域亦是如此。中国读者对南非文学存在过度强调种族隔离、暴力创伤、异域化等刻板印象；南非读者对中国文学 也有共产主义宣传、只关注古代传统等误解。在影视方面，两国电影中也存在对彼此国家人民和文化的刻板描绘。要打破这些刻板印象，就需要两国在文学创作、影视制作等方面加强合作。比如开 展联合写作项目、合拍电影等， 在这些合作中，双方基于项目的顺利推进而相互依赖，从而有机会深入了解对方的文化和价观，打破原有的刻板认知，减少偏见。

        两个群体相互依赖是改善群体关系、减少偏见的重要基础。无论是在种族关系中，还是在不同国家艺术文化交流所涉及的群体关系中， 只有当两个群体建立起相互依赖的关系，才有可能消除敌意、增进信任，打破刻板印象，实现真正的文化交流与融合，构建更加和谐包容的社会环境

China-South Africa Comparative Study: The Role of Interdependence in Breaking Artistic Stereotypes

An examination of stereotypes and misconceptions in the artistic exchanges between China and South Africa reveals the critical importance of interdependence. In the realm of music, both nations face reductive perceptions—Chinese music is often confined to ancient instruments with presumed lack of contemporary relevance, while South African music gets oversimplified as merely traditional African rhythms and tribal sounds. These stereotypes obscure the vibrant diversity of both musical cultures and hinder meaningful exchange. To dismantle such preconceptions, deliberate efforts to foster interdependence are essential. Joint initiatives like collaborative concerts or co-creation projects between musicians can reveal the depth and innovation in each other’s traditions. Through mutual reliance in artistic production, trust replaces bias, allowing authentic appreciation to flourish. Dance faces parallel challenges. South African traditional dance is frequently exoticized as "primitive," while Chinese dance is misperceived as rigid or emotionally sterile. Even contemporary dance forms from both countries struggle for recognition abroad. To transform these narratives, cross-cultural choreography projects could integrate elements from both traditions. The very process of co-creation—navigating artistic differences, solving logistical hurdles—would necessitate interdependence, compelling participants to move beyond caricatures toward genuine mutual respect. Literary and cinematic exchanges suffer similarly. South African literature is often reduced internationally to narratives of apartheid and trauma, while Chinese works face dismissive labels like "state propaganda" or "stuck in antiquity." Film industries in both countries perpetuate shallow portrayals of each other’s societies. Remedy lies in collaborative storytelling: joint publishing ventures or co-produced films would force creators to engage deeply with nuanced realities. When success depends on bridging perspectives, stereotypes collapse under the weight of shared creative purpose. Conclusion Interdependence is the bedrock for transforming intergroup dynamics—whether in racial relations or cross-national artistic dialogue. For China and South Africa, strategic collaboration across arts sectors can replace mistrust with reciprocity. Only through sustained interdependence can we dissolve the fiction of "the other," paving the way for cultural fusion and inclusive societies.
